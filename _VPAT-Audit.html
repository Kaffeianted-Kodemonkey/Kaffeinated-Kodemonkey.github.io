What is a VPAT Audit

A VPAT (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) Audit is a structured evaluation that assesses how well your website meets established accessibility standards. The audit results in a VPAT document, a formal report detailing your site’s compliance with key guidelines, including WCAG, Section 508, and EN 301 549.

For organizations that need to demonstrate accessibility, especially when responding to Requests for Proposals (RFPs), a VPAT Audit is essential. Many government agencies, educational institutions, and corporations require a VPAT to ensure digital products or services are accessible.

Conducting a VPAT Audit identifies gaps in accessibility, helping you develop a strategic roadmap for improvement. Particularly valuable for organizations engaging in federal contracts, a VPAT demonstrates compliance with Section 508 and is vital for showing commitment to accessibility.

What’s Included in the VPAT Audit?

Our VPAT Audit provides a thorough evaluation to assess and enhance your website’s accessibility. Key components include:

  • WCAG, Section 508, and EN 301 549 Evaluation: Comprehensive testing against these standards to identify any accessibility barriers.
  • Detailed Compliance Report: A structured report outlining your site’s current compliance status, including specific areas that may need improvement.
  • Accessibility Barriers Identification: Pinpoints elements that might hinder users with disabilities, ensuring you have a clear understanding of any challenges.
  • Actionable Improvement Plan: A step-by-step roadmap to address identified gaps, helping you meet accessibility standards effectively.
  • Enhanced User Experience Recommendations: Tailored suggestions for making your site more user-friendly and inclusive, ensuring every visitor has a positive experience.

The Benefits of an VPAT Audit

A VPAT Audit is more than just a compliance measure. Here’s how it benefits your organization:

  • Legal Protection: Ensures your website meets federal and international standards, helping to avoid potential legal issues or fines.
  • Enhanced User Experience: Improves usability for all visitors, including those with disabilities, which fosters higher engagement and satisfaction.
  • SEO Advantages: Accessible websites often perform better in search engines, boosting your site’s visibility and reach.

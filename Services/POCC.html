Why Compliance Matters

The Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) requires equal digital access for public entities (Title II) and private businesses (Title III). Colorado's HB21-1110 mandates WCAG 2.1 AA for state/local sites, with full enforcement since July 2025—exposing non-compliant agencies to OIT audits and fines up to $3,500/violation. Federally, Section 508 demands WCAG 2.0 AA for agencies/contractors, while the DOJ’s 2024 Web Rule (effective April 2026 for Title II) aligns with WCAG 2.1 AA. Our POCC provides ironclad documentation to defend against the 3,000+ ADA website lawsuits filed in 2022, proving your commitment to standards.

What is a POCC?

A Proof of Compliance Certificate (POCC) officially validates that your website or digital product meets HB21-1110, ADA, WCAG 2.2, and Section 508 standards for full usability. Issued after our DHS Trusted Tester audits, it includes VPATs for RFPs/contracts and a Disability Impact Score (e.g., 98% conformance rating) to benchmark your site's performance. Display it as a compliance badge for regulators, clients, and search engines—strengthening your position in bids and reducing audit exposure.

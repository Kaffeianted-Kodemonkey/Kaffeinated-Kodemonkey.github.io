What is a VPAT Audit

A VPAT (Voluntary Product Accessibility Template) Audit is a specialized evaluation that helps you assess how well your website meets accessibility standards. The VPAT document is a formal report that outlines your site’s compliance with key accessibility guidelines, including WCAG, Section 508, and EN 301 549. A VPAT Audit involves a detailed review of your website's digital accessibility and provides a transparent, structured assessment of where your site stands in terms of compliance.

This audit is crucial for organizations that need to demonstrate their commitment to accessibility, especially when responding to Requests for Proposals (RFPs). Many government agencies, educational institutions, and large corporations require a VPAT to ensure that the digital products or services they purchase are fully accessible.

Conducting a VPAT Audit not only identifies areas where your site may not meet required standards but also provides a strategic roadmap for addressing these gaps. This can be particularly important when working with government entities, as compliance with Section 508 is mandatory for federal contracts, and the VPAT is a necessary document for proving compliance.

Ultimately, a VPAT Audit empowers your organization with the information needed to address accessibility barriers and ensures that you can confidently report your site’s compliance to stakeholders or regulatory bodies.

The Benefits of an VPAT Audit

An VPAT Audit is more than just a compliance check. It provides legal protection by ensuring your website meets federal and international accessibility laws, which can help avoid potential lawsuits or penalties. Additionally, it creates an opportunity to enhance your website’s user experience. An accessible website improves usability for all visitors, including those with disabilities, and fosters greater engagement and satisfaction.

Beyond accessibility, an inclusive website can improve your search engine optimization (SEO) rankings. Accessibility and SEO often go hand in hand, and improving your site’s accessibility can contribute to higher visibility and better search engine performance.

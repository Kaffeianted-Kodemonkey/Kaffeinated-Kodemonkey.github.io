  • What is web accessibility, and why is it important?

    Web accessibility ensures that websites are usable by everyone, including people with disabilities. It’s important because it enables equal access to digital information, improves the user experience, and helps your site comply with ADA and WCAG standards.

  • What does it mean to be ADA compliant?

    ADA compliance means that your website adheres to the guidelines set by the Americans with Disabilities Act, ensuring that individuals with disabilities can access and navigate your site effectively. This includes following WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) to meet accessibility standards.

  • What is the WCAG, and why should I care?

    The WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) provides a set of standards to make web content more accessible to people with disabilities. Adhering to WCAG not only helps ensure legal compliance but also broadens your audience and boosts SEO.

  • What is a Proof of Compliance (POC) Certificate?

    A Proof of Compliance (POC) Certificate verifies that your website meets ADA and WCAG accessibility standards. It signals your commitment to inclusivity and reduces the risk of non-compliance.

  • How does accessibility impact SEO?

    Accessible websites tend to rank higher in search engine results. By following accessibility best practices, you improve site structure, readability, and overall user experience—all factors that positively impact SEO.
